Maryam Mirzakhani Theoretical Mathematician and Winner of a Fields Medal

1977-2017

Who? Maryam Mirzakhani

What Field? Mathematics

Where? Tehran, Iran/California, USA

How Did She Get Here? Maryam received a Bachelor’s Degree in Mathematics from the Sharif University of Technology in Tehran, then moved to America to pursue a PhD in Mathematics at Harvard University in California.

Notable Career Aspects: Maryam chose to research at Stanford University, while also working as a professor there. She also specializes in extremely complex theoretical mathematics, such as moduli spaces, Teichmüller theory, hyperbolic geometry, Ergodic theory and symplectic geometry. She was commended for bringing a new perspective to solving complex problems, by using a variety of creative approaches to solving them.


"I think it's rarely about what you actually learn in class . . . it's mostly about things that you stay motivated to go and continue to do on your own."

MARYAM MIRZAKHANI

Press Conference after winning Fields Medal

Why We Love Her: Not only was Maryam a brilliant mathematician, but also an extremely creative woman. Although she originally intended on being a writer, she discovered her passion for mathematics and then to pursue that. However, she never lost her creative streak, as many of the problems that were previously unsolvable, Maryam solved through a combination of extremely deep thinking alongside some abstract thinking. This absolute BOSS was also the first, and currently only, woman to have ever been awarded a prestigious Fields Medal, which is basically the equivalent of a Nobel Prize in Mathematics, wow!
