Are you...

At school?

BBC Science Bitesize: https://www.bbc.com/bitesize/subjects/z7nygk7 BBC Bitesize - KS2 Science https://www.bbc.com/bitesize/subjects/z2pfb9q KS2 Science learning resources for adults, children, parents and teachers organised by topic. BBC Teach - Terrific Scientific https://www.bbc.com/teach/terrific-scientific Terrific Scientific brings science to the heart of your teaching with fantastic investigations and exciting resources linked to the curriculum. BBC Bitesize - KS3 Science https://www.bbc.com/bitesize/subjects/zng4d2p KS3 Science learning resources for adults, children, parents and teachers organised by topic. https://stemettes.org/ Stemettes, aimed for women aged 5-22 host a variety of free events to encourage women to pursue a career in STEM

At university?

Equate Scotland: https://equatescotland.org.uk/ An organisation committed to promote and encourage the advancement of women in STEM, with lots of free resources, conferences, advice, and internships

A professional?

Athena Swan Charter: https://www.ecu.ac.uk/equality-charters/athena-swan/ Advance HE’s Athena SWAN Charter was established in 2005 to encourage and recognise commitment to advancing the careers of women in science, technology, engineering, maths and medicine (STEMM) employment in higher education and research.

Generally interested?

STEM Ambassadors: https://www.stem.org.uk/stem-ambassadors STEM Ambassadors are volunteers from a wide range of science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM) related jobs and disciplines across the UK. They offer their time and enthusiasm to help bring STEM subjects to life and demonstrate the value of them in life and careers. BBC Science: https://www.bbc.co.uk/science Women in STEM: https://www.womeninstem.co.uk/ The Women in STEM campaign is an initiative to showcase the different opportunities for women in the STEM sector, whilst also trying to bridge the stereotypes of STEM subjects – with the ultimate goal being to close the gender gap. Women in Global Science and Technology: http://wisat.org/
